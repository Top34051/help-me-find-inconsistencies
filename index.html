Research Study on Inconsistency Detection in Wikipedia

Thank you for participating in this research study on inconsistency detection on Wikipedia. In this study, we will ask you to find inconsistencies in the English Wikipedia with and without the help of our chrome extension which will be provided to you. Please read the following details carefully.

Help Me Find Inconsistencies!

Factual inconsistencies in Wikipedia articles occur when information conflicts between different pages. This can happen when different editors work independently on related topics, or when one article is updated with new information while another isn't. Identifying these inconsistencies is the first step toward resolving them.

However, even simple conflicts can be challenging to spot, so we have developed a chrome extension that highlights potential inconsistencies in Wikipedia articles for editors to review.

Study Procedure

The study will be split into two parts. In the first part, you will be given a list of Wikipedia articles and asked to find inconsistencies in them without the help of the Chrome extension. In the second part, you will be given a different list of articles and asked to find inconsistencies again, but this time with the help of the Chrome extension.

Part 1: Without the Chrome Extension

  1. You will be given a list of paragraphs from the articles. You will be asked to identify the inconsistencies in the paragraphs. Each paragraph given to you will look like this example:
    2. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top-level_domain#History
  2. You may use any resources, including the internet, to help you identify inconsistencies. However, you must only report inconsistencies between the provided paragraphs and other content found within Wikipedia articles.
  3. You will complete a form where you'll document each inconsistency you discover and track how much time you spent finding them.

Part 2: With the Chrome Extension

  1. Install the Chrome extension as described in the Installation section.
  2. You will be given another list of paragraphs from the articles. You will be asked to identify the inconsistencies in them with the help of the Chrome extension.

    3. Note: The chrome extension will highlight potential inconsistencies in the paragraphs. You can click on the highlighted text to see why it is potentially inconsistent. This helps you identify inconsistencies more quickly. However, please note that these flagged inconsistencies may not always represent actual errors.

  3. Again, you may use any resources, including the internet, along with the Chrome extension to help you identify inconsistencies. You must only report inconsistencies between the provided paragraphs and other content found within Wikipedia articles.
  4. Give feedback on the statements that the chrome extension flags as potentially inconsistent whether they are actually inconsistent or not.
  5. After you have completed the task, you will complete a form where you'll document each inconsistency you discover and track how much time you spent finding them.

Some participants may be asked to complete the parts in a different order (i.e. Part 2 first, then Part 1). You will be notified of this beforehand.

Chrome Extension Installation

  1. Download this zip file: help-me-find-inconsistencies.zip
  2. Unzip the file, and you will see a folder called help-me-find-inconsistencies.
  3. Open Chrome and navigate to chrome://extensions.
  4. Enable "Developer mode" if it is not already enabled.
  5. Click "Load unpacked" and select the help-me-find-inconsistencies folder.
  6. Reload the page after installing the extension and you should see the extension icon in the top right corner of the page! Please let us know if you have any issues.

Time Involvement

In each part, you will review 3 paragraphs. We estimate that each paragraph will take up to 20 minutes to review. If it takes you longer than 20 minutes to review a paragraph, you may stop participating in that paragraph and specify this in the form. Therefore, each part should take between 45-60 minutes to complete.
